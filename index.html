Mathematics

Mathematics is a field of study that discovers and organizes methods, theories, and theorems that are developed and proved for the needs of empirical science and mathematics itself.

Mathematics can be classified into following two types

Pure Mathematics

This branch focuses on understanding mathematical ideas, patterns, and structures for their own sake. It is more about theory, logic, and abstraction than direct real-world application.

Pure mathesmatics can be divided into two major parts:

Discrete Mathematics

Discrete mathematics deals with separate, countable objects. It does not involve continuous change but rather distinct values.

It includes:

Arithmetic

This is where everything begins. Beyond basic operations, deeper arithmetic studies:

  • Divisibility rules
  • Prime numbers
  • Modular arithmetic (clock math)
Boolean Algebra

This is the math of true/false values.

  • Built on logical operations: AND, OR, NOT
  • Foundation of digital circuits and programming
Set Theory

This is the language of all mathematics.

  • Defines collections (sets), subsets, unions, intersections
  • Builds the foundation for functions, numbers, and structures
Combinatorics

This is the art of counting cleverly.

  • Permutation
  • Combination
Logic

Logic studies valid reasoning.

  • Propositions and truth values
  • Proof techniques (direct, contradiction, induction)
Number Theory

Often called the “queen of mathematics.”

  • Studies integers and their properties
  • Topics: primes, divisibility, Diophantine equations
Graph Theory

Studies networks.

  • Vertices (points) and edges (connections)
  • Paths, cycles, connectivity
Discrete Statistics

Deals with countable data.

  • Examples: number of students, number of defects
Algorithms

Step-by-step methods to solve problems.

  • Efficiency (time, memory)
  • Sorting, searching, optimization

Continuous Mathematics

Continuous mathematics deals with smooth, continuous quantities, such as space, motion, and change.

It includes:

Linear Algebra

The math of space and direction.

  • Vectors → quantities with direction
  • Matrices → transformations
Abstract (Modern) Algebra

Studies structures and symmetry.

  • Groups → symmetry operations
  • Rings & fields → number systems
Geometry

Study of space and shape.

  • Euclidean Geometry
  • Non-Euclidean Geometry
Topology

Known as “rubber-sheet geometry.”

  • Focuses on properties unchanged by stretching
  • A coffee cup and donut are equivalent here
Trigonometry

Study of angles and periodic behavior.

  • Sine, cosine, tangent
  • Wave patterns
Calculus

The math of change and accumulation.

  • Differential Calculus → rates of change
  • Integral Calculus → total accumulation
Continuous Statistics

Deals with continuous data.

  • Heights, weights, time
  • Probability distributions
Analysis

The rigorous foundation of calculus.

  • Real Analysis
  • Complex Analysis
Measure Theory

Defines what “size” really means.

  • Generalizes length, area, volume
  • Foundation of probability theory

Applied Mathematics

Applied mathematics focuses on using mathematical methods to solve real-world problems.

It includes:

Elementary/Basic Algebra
  • Solving equations
  • Working with functions
Statistics

The science of data.

  • Collecting, organizing, analyzing
  • Making predictions
Probability

The math of uncertainty.

  • Likelihood of events
  • Random variables
Mechanics

Studies the motion of objects and the forces acting on them.

Statics

Studies objects at rest or in equilibrium.

Dynamics

Studies objects in motion under forces. It has two parts:

Kinematics

Describes motion only (position, velocity, acceleration)

Kinetics

Studies motion along with forces causing it

Computational Mathematics

Solving math problems using numerical methods.

  • Approximation instead of exact solutions
  • Algorithms for large systems
Mathematical Physics

Applies math directly to physical laws

  • Differential equations
  • Field theory

Intermediate Algebra

Intermediate algebra lies between basic and advanced mathematics which includes:

  • Functions
  • Sequence and series
  • Quadratic equations
  • Comoplex numbers

This acts as a bridge connecting elementary concepts to higher-level mathematics
